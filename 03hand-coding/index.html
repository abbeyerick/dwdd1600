About Abbey Erickson

Hi, my name is Abbey, and this is my little photography site. I got into film photography after feeling burnt out and uninspired with digital. I absolutely hated the need and expectation to edit my digital photos; I wanted to take photos as they are. That’s when I discovered film photography through social media creators. I bought my Canon AE-1 with my first paycheck, a bit on impulse, but it all worked out in the end.

I’ve had a lot of trial and error learning film in a digital era. I love the ability to intentionally capture moments and the excitement of seeing how they turn out after development. I let the film and its grain add the editing — that’s where Her Film Grain comes in.

Film Photography Terms & Definitions

Grain
The random physical texture made from small metallic silver particles found on processed photographic celluloid.
35 mm
A small-format photographic film used in single-lens reflex cameras (SLRs), film cameras, rangefinders, and disposable cameras. The number 35 refers to the total width of the film, including its perforated edges.
Manual camera
A camera that allows the user to manually adjust the camera's settings, such as aperture, shutter speed, and ISO.
